Senior Design Project

Designing and machining various parts for the development of the High-contrast imager for Complex Aperture Telescopes (HiCAT)
Back to resume and portfolio

Project Details

My senior design team aims to design and build various mechanical components needed by the researchers to test their imaging system. Unlike most other senior design projects, we work on a lot of small projects rather than one big project. The parts we design are used in a clean room with very sensitive optical equipment, which provides challenges in precision and material choices. Working on this project has refined our design skills, as well as greatly increased our machining knowledge and skills as well. Solidworks is used to help visualize how our designs interface with various commercial parts, and to help visualize how our parts fit on the optical table.

Example Part

We do a wide variety of projects for the researchers depending on their current needs. This is an example of one of the designs we created. They wanted an optical filter could attach to a rotation motor, and also wanted the capability of swapping out the optical component easily. We decided to obtain the swapping capability by using a kinematic attachment with magnets, which allows one to attach two plates together with great repeatability and precision. We found commercial kinematic components that could hold the optics needed utilizing spanner rings. We designed a part to interface the motor with the commercial parts, and then verified that our part fit on the testbed and that the motors load limits weren't exceeded. We then machined the interfacing part and verified that the design worked as intended.

Video on testbed

A researcher demonstrates attaching the optical filter utilizing the our design. With the kinematic mechanism, attaching a component and removing a component is extremely simple.

Skills utilized

I utilize machining skills in order to manufacture the parts needed to the correct precisions. In addition, this project has allowed me to utilize CAD and design skills, and more specifically, how to design better with machining in mind.
